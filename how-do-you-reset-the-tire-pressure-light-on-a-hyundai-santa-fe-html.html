    Answer

    Turn the ignition key to the “On” position without starting the vehicle. Press and hold the TPMS reset button for three seconds or until the light blinks three times, and then release the button. Start the vehicle and let it run for 20 minutes to allow the sensor to be reset and calibrated. The reset button for the tyre pressure monitor is normally located under the steering wheel in most vehicles.

    Specifically, how does one go about resetting the TPMS on a Hyundai Santa Fe?

    For around 10 minutes, drive the automobile at 50 mph. Turn the ignition key to the “On” position without starting the vehicle. Press and hold the TPMS reset button for three seconds or until the light blinks three times, and then release the button. Start the vehicle and let it run for 20 minutes to allow the sensor to be reset and calibrated.

    Is it possible that cold temperatures will cause the low tyre pressure indicator to illuminate?

    Your tyre pressure will fall by about 1 psi for every 10 degrees Fahrenheit as the temperature of the outside air lowers. When the temperature lowers overnight and the low 40s are reached, your tyre pressure will drop by 5 psi, which will very probably result in the tyre pressure warning light being activated.

    What is the root reason of TPMS failure?

    The majority of tyre pressure monitoring system failures are caused by tyre pressure sensors that have reached the end of their useful life. Most tyre pressure sensors have a service life of 5 to 7 years, which is average for the batteries found within the sensors. Tire pressure sensors may potentially fail as a consequence of corrosion on the valve stem or inside the valve stem itself.
