    Swiss tennis player Stanislas Wawrinka was born on March 28, 1985. In January 2014, he attained a singles career-high from ATP (Association of Tennis Professionals) ranking of No. 3 for the first time. The three Grand Slam titles he's won—the 2014 Australian Open, the 2015 French Open, and the 2016 US Open—are among his career high points. On each of those three occasions, he defeated the player ranked No. 1 in the world in the final.

    Other accomplishments include making the 2017 French Open final, taking home the 2014 Monte-Carlo Masters championship, and making it to the final of three further Masters tournaments (2008 Rome, 2013 Madrid, and 2017 Indian Wells). In addition to helping the Swiss team win the 2014 Davis Cup, Wawrinka earned the gold medal in doubles at the 2008 Beijing Olympics alongside Roger Federer.

    On February 27, 2023, Wawrinka reentered the top 100 rankings at position 97. He defeated the 26th seed Miomir Kecmanovic and the 7th seed Holger Rune in the 2023 BNP Paribas Open while using a protected ranking to go to the fourth round of a Masters tournament for the first time since 2020, earning his 550th career victory in the process.

    Wawrinka beat Emil Ruusuvuori and Tomás Martín Etcheverry at the 2023 Wimbledon Championships to go to a third-round match against Novak Djokovic, but he lost in straight sets. He joined up with Dominic Stricker at the Swiss Open in Gstaad and eventually won the tournament.

    Then, at the 2023 Croatia Open, Wawrinka defeated Lorenzo Sonego, Roberto Carballes Baena, Federico Coria, and Filip Misolic. He prevailed in a tiebreaker to take the opening set, but Australian Alexei Popyrin eventually defeated him in three sets. On July 31, 2023, he rose to World No. 49 as a result of making the final.
