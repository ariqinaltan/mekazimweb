    Experience New York from a new vantage point on The Mark Sailboat this summer. If you’re taking your first city break in awhile—or enjoying a staycation closer to home—The Mark Hotel is now booking private charters for guests, friends, and Upper East Side neighbors on its ultra luxurious sailboat.

    Featuring a solid mahogany hull, Indian teak decks, and the hotel’s signature black and white stripes on its customized sail and cushions, the 70-foot Herreshoff sailboat is an uptown mood. It was originally built in 1921 by Nathanael Greene Herreshoff, a naval architect who designed six America’s Cup defending yachts and whose designs are are known for balancing style and scientific engineering.

    Courtesy of The Mark Hotel

    The Mark Sailboat approaching Manhatttan

    The vessel, which comes fully staffed with a captain, two crew members, and two servers, sets sail for a two-hour journey into New York Harbor from Tribeca’s North Cove Marina (adjacent to Westfield World Trade Center). The route offers breathtaking views of the lower Manhattan skyline and the Statue of Liberty.

    Angela Pham

    Jean-Georges Vongerichten takes the wheel

    Along the way, you can enjoy Côtes de Provence rosé and light bites like tuna tartare crostini, spiced chicken samosas with cilantro yogurt, oysters on the half shell, and colorful crudité from a menu created by The Mark’s resident chef Jean-Georges Vongerichten, who is himself an avid sailor.

    Angela Pham

    Colorful crudité

    The Mark Sailboat can accommodate up to 25 guests, making it the perfect venue for an intimate open air wedding. For those looking to take the plunge, The Mark’s Wedding Officiant can be brought on board for an unforgettable ceremony on the water.
